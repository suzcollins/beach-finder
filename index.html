Our Favorites

Good Harbor Beach, Gloucester, MA

Soft white sand and shinning blue water. Good Harbor is one of the most popular beaches in Gloucster. During low tide, one is able to walk to Salt Island by the land bridge. Restrooms and showers are open from 8:00 am to 6:00 pm each day. Parking (buses prohibited,) is available during the week at a rate of $25 per vehicle, and on the weekends and holidays at $30 per vehicle. Non-resident parking is limited so arrive very early in order to get a parking space, especially during the summer months. Gates open at 8:00 am and are locked at 9:00 pm. Lifeguards are on duty daily from 9:00 am to 5:00 pm from Memorial Day weekend through Labor Day weekend. Food stands will also be available during the summer so drinks and snacks may be purchased. Boogie boarding is allowed during all hours and surfing allowed after 5PM. The beach is handicapped accessible.

Ogunquit Beach, Ogunquit, ME

Ogunquit Beach is located in the town of Ogunquit, Maine. This beach is a boogie boarders, paddleboarders, and surfers paradise. With beautiful waves and crystal clear water, this is the ideal beach for families and anyone looking for a great time. A river meets with the beach which is perfect if you have a water tube to float down the river or catch crabs. Parking is tough during the summer months and can get pricey depending on which lot you go to. You cannot have a dog during the summer months but can off season. Ogunquit beach does have a few quick spots to eat at but a five minute walk away from the beach is the town of Ogunquit with a bunch of my personal favorite spots to eat. Loveshack Juicery is the best if you're looking for a healthy smoothie bowl or juice. Cornerstone if you're looking for artisian pizza and amazing homemade pretzels. Bread and Roses is perfect for a cup of iced coffee or any pastry. Rentals for SUP and surfboards are available at Liquid Dreams in Ogunquit or York and you can take lessons too. Restrooms and outdoor showers are available as well next to the beach. Lifeguards are on duty between June 20th and Labor Day. Beware of the seagulls, they are aggressive.

Goose Rocks Beach , Kennebunkport, ME

Goose Rocks Beach is located right outside of Kennebunkport being the being the biggest beach in Kennebunkport at 3 miles long. This beach is perfect, the waves aren't the biggest, but the soft sand and beautiful sunset make up for it. Goose Rocks Beach is super busy in the summer. Parking is $15 for a day pass at the local store on the beach and parking is located all along the beach. There are many more resturants located in the town of Kennebunkport but located right on the beach are many seafood resturants. The Tides which is amazing resturant and Inn. There is also a general store where you can buy sandwhiches which are really good, pizza, chips and many different types of drinks. Goose Rocks Beach is an amazing beach in Kenebunkport and is amazing for a family day at the beach. There are no lifeguards located at the beach.
